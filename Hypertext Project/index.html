Spanglish Sin Bumbo

I’m from Brooklyn,
where the streets hum salsa
and the summer smells like azúcar
melting on piragua ice.
Mami calls me pa’ dentro—
“Ponte las chancletas, nene!”
but I’m out watching
los viejitos play domino
on fold-out tables by the curb,
tiles clicking like heartbeat drums
under stoop-light sunsets.
My voice?
It dances Spanglish,
soft like coquito,
strong like abuelita’s regaño.
